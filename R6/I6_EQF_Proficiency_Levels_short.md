# EQF Proficiency Levels

| EQF | Level       | Description                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| --- | ----------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | Supervised  | Basic general knowledge; basic skills for simple tasks; works under direct supervision in a structured context.                                                                                                                                                                         |
| 2   | Operational | Basic factual knowledge; uses relevant information to carry out tasks and solve routine problems; works under supervision with some autonomy.                                                                                                                                           |
| 3   | Qualified   | Knowledge of facts, principles and processes; selects and applies basic methods and tools; takes responsibility for task completion and adapts behaviour to circumstances.                                                                                                              |
| 4   | Supervising | Factual and theoretical knowledge in broad contexts; generates solutions to specific problems; self-manages within predictable guidelines and supervises routine work of others.                                                                                                        |
| 5   | Managing    | Comprehensive specialised knowledge aware of its own boundaries; develops creative solutions to abstract problems; manages and supervises in unpredictable contexts; reviews and develops performance of self and others.                                                               |
| 6   | Accountable | Advanced knowledge with critical understanding of theories and principles; demonstrates mastery and innovation on complex problems; manages projects and takes responsibility for professional development of individuals and groups.                                                   |
| 7   | Strategic   | Highly specialised knowledge at the forefront of a field; integrates knowledge across fields; manages and transforms complex, unpredictable contexts requiring new strategic approaches; contributes to professional knowledge and reviews strategic team performance.                  |
| 8   | Visionary   | Knowledge at the most advanced frontier and at the interface between fields; most advanced skills for solving critical problems in research and innovation; demonstrates substantial authority, autonomy and sustained commitment to developing new ideas at the forefront of practice. |
